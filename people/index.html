Meet the crew ↓

01

Everyone, together

The whole crew

The shots where the whole family is in one frame — the ones that end up framed on a wall. From the Mediterranean south to the golden stones of the Old City, this is the crew that did it all: together, the whole way up.

“You don't remember the miles. You remember who was sitting next to you.”
02

Warren

Dad · keeper of the tefillin

The one who wraps tefillin on a moving train because the prayer can't wait for the platform. Warren is the quiet centre of gravity on this trip — first to the Wall, hand always on the stone, and the one who stood longest at every memorial in the south. Reverence, with a boarding pass.

“He'd tell you the trip was about the places. It was always about the people — the living and the remembered.”
03

Mom & the co-pilot

The unstoppable travel duo

Every great trip has a pair who make the getting-there half the fun. Mom and her daughter rode every tram, every train, and every escalator-into-the-mountain shoulder to shoulder — window seats claimed, snacks acquired, morale management handled. The heart of the operation.

“Give them a tram, a snack and a window, and they'd ride to the ends of the earth.”
04

The daughter

Co-pilot · flag-bearer · dad's shadow

One half of the travel duo, and the other half of a dad-and-daughter double act that turned up at the Wall right on cue. She's in more frames than anyone — because she was into all of it, from the front of every tram to the foot of the last wall of the Temple.

“The best souvenir she brought home wasn't a thing. It was the whole week.”
05

Amler

The guide who carried the hardest day

Not family by blood, but family for a day. Amler led the Sacharowitzes through the Gaza-envelope region — Sderot, the Tkuma car wall, the Ashkelon coast, and the Nova memorial at Re'im — navigating the checkpoints and, more importantly, the weight of it. Some guides show you a place. Amler helped a family hold one.

“The right guide doesn't just get you there. He gets you through it.”

That's the whole crew

Four of them and a guide, one week, and a country's worth of trains, trams, stones and sea. The places were extraordinary. The company was the point.
